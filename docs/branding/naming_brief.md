# Naming Brief

## One-Sentence Positioning
Software that creates legally robust ancillary-cost settlements (Nebenkosten-/Betriebskostenabrechnungen) for landlords and property managers in Germany and Switzerland.

## Primary Audience
- Small and mid-size private landlords in Germany.
- Property managers in Germany and Swiss German-speaking cantons.

## Secondary Audience
- Swiss users outside Swiss German regions.
- Potential later EU/international expansion users.

## Trust Attributes We Need the Name to Signal
- Reliability and legal defensibility.
- Professional, administrative seriousness.
- Clarity without being purely descriptive.
- Neutral and non-hyped tone.

## Portability Constraints
- Must be pronounceable in German and English.
- Prefer 2-4 syllables, low typo risk.
- Avoid umlaut-only spellings as canonical brand form.
- Should not lock product permanently to DACH-only terminology.

## Forbidden or Discouraged Patterns
- High-risk descriptive compounds as primary brand: `Nebenkosten*`, `Umlage*`, `Abrechnung*`.
- Overcrowded real-estate prefixes: `Immo*` unless paired with highly distinctive stem.
- Names too close to known incumbents (App Store, domain, social, package ecosystems).
- Terms likely to be interpreted as purely generic product descriptions.

## Legal-Risk Assumptions (Internal Screening)
- Internal checks are pre-screen only and do not guarantee freedom to operate.
- Hard blocker: exact trademark collisions in target classes/territories.
- Soft blocker: high phonetic/visual similarity to active market players.
- Final legal decision requires counsel review.

## Decision Rule
- Candidate can advance only if it passes hard legal and collision gates, reaches score >= 75/100, and meets DE/CH comprehension/trust thresholds.
